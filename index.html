EXPLORE PRAJNA

PRAJNA (Think, Create, Celebrate) marks the third edition of the Annual State-Level Intercollegiate fest organized by Poornaprajna College (Autonomous), Udupi. Rooted in the idea of Prajna, wisdom and awareness, the fest brings together students from various institutions in an environment of learning, creativity, and meaningful competition.


Drawing inspiration from the life and philosophy of Lord Krishna, this year’s fest reflects the balance between intellect, strategy, and artistic expression.


The fest unfolds over two days, with academic events on Day 1 that engage participants in intellectual challenges across disciplines, followed by cultural events on Day 2 featuring music, dance, and artistic performances. Prajna 2026 aims to create a vibrant space where ideas, creativity, and collaboration come together.


GENERAL GUIDELINE

  • All competitions are open to undergraduate students only

  • Participants must carry their original college ID card, which is mandatory for entry

  • Only students are allowed to participate in the events. Non-participants will not be permitted in the event venue

  • The college permission letter must be duly signed and sealed by the Head of the Institution

  • Registration fees for all academic is ₹100 per event

  • Registration fees for all cultural is ₹200 per event

  • Registration fees for Pencil Sketch is ₹100

  • Each college is allowed to send only one team for academic and cultural events respectively

  • A participant may take part in a maximum of two cultural events

  • Day 1 (April 6th) will feature academic events

  • Day 2 (April 7th) will feature cultural events

  • All participants must report to the registration desk at 8:30 A.M. on the day of the fest

  • Participants must contact the event coordinators to confirm their slots. Once the slots are fixed, changes will not be entertained

  • Participants must bring all required materials for their respective events

  • Pen drives must be submitted at least 30 minutes before the event, containing only the required song/file/PPT

  • Any additional requirements will be specified under the respective event rules

  • Outside food is not allowed. Food will be provided for participants and the faculty in charge

  • The college will not provide accommodation for participants

  • Decent dress code has to be followed by all participants

  • Any form of misconduct or Indecency will lead to the immediate disqualification of the concerned college from all events

  • The Judges’ decisions for all events are final and binding

  • The use of pet animals, fire, sharp weapons, or any harmful objects is strictly prohibited

  • The order of performances for all cultural events will be determined through a random draw (lot picking) conducted prior to the event, and participants must adhere to the sequence

ABOUT

Poornaprajna College is one of the educational institutions run by Udupi Shree Adamaru Matha Education Council formerly headed by H. H. Shree Shree Vibudhesha Theertha Swamiji of Shree Adamaru Matha. Now, the Council is functioning under the leadership of H. H. Shree Shree Eeshapriya Theertha Swamiji of Shree Adamaru Matha.

Poornaprajna College was founded in 1960 by the Swamijis of eight Mathas of Udupi. But, in February 1962, with a view to streamline the day-to-day working of the institution, its maintenance and governance was handed over to Adamaru Matha Education Council, a registered society functioning under the leadership of H. H. Shree Shree Vibudhesha Theertha Swamiji of Adamaru Matha.

Poornaprajna College, popularly known as PPC, is situated in a vast green campus at the heart of Udupi city. It is at a short distance of 1 KM from Udupi Bus Stand, 4 KMs from Udupi Railway Station and about 45 KMs from Mangalore Airport.
