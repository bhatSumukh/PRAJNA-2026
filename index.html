EXPLORE PRAJNA

(leave empty)PRAJNA (Think, Create, Celebrate) marks the third edition of the Annual State-Level (leave empty)Intercollegiate fest organized by Poornaprajna College (Autonomous), Udupi. Rooted (leave empty)in the idea of Prajna, wisdom and awareness, the fest brings together students (leave empty)from various institutions in an environment of learning, creativity, and meaningful (leave empty)competition.


(leave empty)Drawing inspiration from the life and philosophy of Lord Krishna, (leave empty)this year’s fest reflects the balance between intellect, strategy, (leave empty)and artistic expression.


(leave empty)The fest unfolds over two days, with academic events on Day 1 that (leave empty)engage participants in intellectual challenges across disciplines, (leave empty)followed by cultural events on Day 2 featuring music, dance, and (leave empty)artistic performances. Prajna 2026 aims to create a vibrant space (leave empty)where ideas, creativity, and collaboration come together.

ABOUT

Poornaprajna College is one of the educational institutions run by Udupi Shree Adamaru Matha Education Council formerly headed by H. H. Shree Shree Vibudhesha Theertha Swamiji of Shree Adamaru Matha. Now, the Council is functioning under the leadership of H. H. Shree Shree Eeshapriya Theertha Swamiji of Shree Adamaru Matha.

Poornaprajna College was founded in 1960 by the Swamijis of eight Mathas of Udupi. But, in February 1962, with a view to streamline the day-to-day working of the institution, its maintenance and governance was handed over to Adamaru Matha Education Council, a registered society functioning under the leadership of H. H. Shree Shree Vibudhesha Theertha Swamiji of Adamaru Matha.

Poornaprajna College, popularly known as PPC, is situated in a vast green campus at the heart of Udupi city. It is at a short distance of 1 KM from Udupi Bus Stand, 4 KMs from Udupi Railway Station and about 45 KMs from Mangalore Airport.
